## About Me

I am a GIS Researcher at the **Ackerman Centre for Holocaust Studies, University of Texas at Dallas**, working under Prof. Fang Qiu. My work bridges historical GIS, digital humanities, and spatial data science — most notably through large-scale geocoding and spatial analysis of 168,119 Dachau concentration camp prisoner records (1933–1945).

I am completing an **MS in Geospatial Information Sciences at UTD** and serve as a **Part-time Lecturer at the University of Guyana**, teaching GIS 5203: Geospatial Analyses for Crime and Criminal Activities.

My research spans remote sensing, above-ground biomass estimation, SAR-based flood analysis, and GeoAI — with manuscripts targeting high-impact journals including *Remote Sensing of Environment*.

## Featured Projects

**Dachau Concentration Camp GIS (2024–Present)**
Spatial analysis of 168,119 prisoner records across 12 years of camp history. Outputs include an ArcGIS StoryMap, interactive dashboard, and temporal arrival analysis.
[View StoryMap →](https://arcg.is/1TmTy4)

**AlphaEarth Above-Ground Biomass Mapping — Texas**
Compared AlphaEarth foundation model embeddings vs. traditional Sentinel-1/2 features across 603,350 GEDI L4A footprints. Best model: XGBoost (R²=0.772, RMSE=23.97 Mg/ha). Target: *Remote Sensing of Environment*.

**Pakistan Flood Soil Memory Analysis**
SAR-derived soil moisture analysis of 2022 and 2025 southern Punjab flood events. Introduced False Recovery Grade (FRG) and a six-class resilience framework. Target: *Remote Sensing of Environment*.
